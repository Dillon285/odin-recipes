Island Bliss - Prawn Salad with Coconut Lime Dressing

Home

Description

This salad features super-fresh flavours and great textures, perfect for when you are a bit over hearty meats and want something light yet fulfilling. The combination of plump prawns, sweet apple, crunch cabbage and vibrant herbs all combed with the richness of a lime coconut dressing is amazing.

Originally this recipe was sourced from the great woman Recipetineats who has quite a wealth of good recipes to follow. Ingredients

  • Ayam 100% natural coconut cream - 270ml
    • Image for reference:

  • 1 lime
  • 1 Cabbage
  • Prawns
  • Granny smith apple
  • Fish sauce
  • Sugar
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Green onion
  • Mint
  • Coriander

Steps

  1. Making the dressing
    1. Empty out the coconut cream into a large cup and leave it in the fridge for at least an hour
    2. Grate some lime zest into the cream alongside two wedges of lime juice
    3. Add fish sauce, sugar, grated garlic and ginger
    4. Mix well until combined
  2. Preparing the salad
    1. Chop up the whole cabbage into inch length portions, image for reference:

    2. Optional: Cut prawns in half so they are easier to eat in bitesized portions
    3. Cut the apple lengthwise and then again to create apple cubed slices, image for reference:

      • Squeeze a lime wedge over the apples and then mix the slices around
  3. Assembling the salad
    1. Combine the sliced cabbage, apples, green onion and a bit of the dressing together in a bowl and toss together
    2. Then add the prawns, mint and coriander to toss once again
  4. Serving the salad
    1. Grab a portion of the salad and place it onto a plate
    2. Add some extra dressing onto the portioned salad
    3. Garnish with some extra coriander and mint alongside lime wedges
  5. Enjoy!

Notes

  • The dressings arn't a necessity, use if you have
  • I found for two people half of half of a cabbage is enough (so a quarter)
  • Is a salad with quite an amount of texture, leaning on the fibrous side
  • Depending on which part of the cabbage you cut will impact the crunchyness of the salad
